## 1. Why This Scenario (Context Anchoring)

The corporate wellness industry in India is a real, active market. GOQii — a Mumbai-based
preventive-healthcare technology company (Founder & CEO: Vishal Gondal) — runs an
enterprise wellness business whose real, publicly documented offerings include:

- **GOQii Corporate Challenge (GCC):** a real inter-corporate fitness event combining
  daily virtual sessions, spot challenges, and a multi-week inter-corporate step
  challenge inside the GOQii app. The 2021–22 edition drew **over 25,000 employees
  across ~40 corporations** (IOCL, HPCL, Tata Sky, Bajaj Allianz, Dr Reddy's,
  Publicis Sapient, etc.). [Source 1, 2]
- **AI-driven smart wearables** (GOQii Smart Vital / Vital Lite) using AI algorithms for
  continuous heart-rate and SpO2 monitoring, synced to the app. [Source 3]
- **1-on-1 human health coaching** via certified coaches and doctors inside the app. [Source 4]
- **India Fit Report:** a real annual health-data study drawn from millions of GOQii app
  users via Health Risk Assessments (HRA). [Source 5]
- A stated business thesis that preventive corporate wellness delivers measurable ROI
  through reduced absenteeism and improved productivity. [Source 6]

**The fictional project below** imagines a vendor (modeled on this business) deploying
such a platform to one new enterprise client. Anchoring the *context* in these real
facts makes the practice realistic while keeping the *project itself* clearly hypothetical.

## 2. Business Case

### 2.1 The Problem / Opportunity
"Meridian Logistics Pvt Ltd" (fictional client) is a 5,000-employee Indian logistics firm
facing rising group-health-insurance premiums, growing sedentary-lifestyle health claims,
and falling employee engagement scores after a shift to hybrid work. Leadership wants a
data-driven preventive-wellness program rather than a one-off perk.

### 2.2 The Proposed Solution
Deploy an AI-powered corporate wellness platform across all 5,000 employees over a
~6-month engagement:
- Distribute AI-enabled smart bands to opted-in employees.
- Onboard employees onto a wellness mobile app (HRA, activity/sleep/vitals tracking).
- Run a flagship inter-team Corporate Challenge to drive engagement.
- Provide AI + human health-coaching nudges ("Dynamic Motivation" style).
- Deliver an HR analytics dashboard tracking engagement, health-risk distribution,
  and absenteeism trends.

### 2.3 Value Proposition & Benefits
- **Quantifiable:** target ≥60% active enrollment, ≥40% sustained 90-day engagement,
  measurable absenteeism reduction, and a downstream case for lower insurance loading.
- **Qualitative:** stronger team morale, employer-brand differentiation, HR data to
  inform future benefits design.

### 2.4 ROI Rationale (Illustrative)
Preventive wellness ROI is framed via avoided costs: reduced absenteeism days,
lower lifestyle-disease claim frequency, and improved productivity — the same logic
GOQii publicly argues for preventive corporate health. [Source 6] *(All figures in the
capstone artifacts are illustrative and self-generated for practice.)*

### 2.5 Stacey Analysis (per Module 1 requirement)
- **Requirements:** moderately agreed (client knows the goal, details emerge) → mid axis.
- **Technology/approach:** largely known (proven platform) but with integration unknowns.
- **Verdict:** **Complicated, leaning Complex** → justifies a **hybrid** approach:
  *predictive* planning for hardware/logistics/contracts, *adaptive (Scrum)* for the
  app-configuration and engagement-feature workstream. This cleanly satisfies the
  capstone's requirement to produce **both** predictive *and* adaptive artifacts.

## 3. Project Scenario Narrative (Reusable Across All 7 Labs)

**Project name:** VitalEdge — Corporate Wellness Platform Deployment for Meridian Logistics

**Vendor (you, the PM team):** "WellSphere Technologies" (fictional, modeled on GOQii's
enterprise wellness business).

**Engagement window:** 1 Aug 2026 – 31 Jan 2027 (~6 months) — *fictional dates.*

**High-level phases (predictive backbone):**
1. **Initiation** — Charter, stakeholder register, kickoff.
2. **Planning** — WBS, network diagram, budget, quality/comms/risk plans.
3. **Procurement & Logistics** — band sourcing, distribution to 5 sites.
4. **Platform Configuration** — app setup, SSO, HRA flows, dashboard *(run as Scrum sprints)*.
5. **Onboarding & Launch** — employee enrollment, training, Corporate Challenge kickoff.
6. **Operate & Monitor** — engagement tracking, status reports, change requests.
7. **Close-Out** — final report, lessons learned, handover to client HR.

**This single narrative feeds every lab:**

| Lab | Artifact | What this scenario provides |
|-----|----------|------------------------------|
| 1 | Project Charter + Stakeholder Register | Sponsor, goal, ROI, RACI roles |

| 3 | Quality + Communications + Risk plans | Data-privacy, adoption, device-logistics risks |
| 4 | User Stories + Product + Sprint Backlog | App-configuration workstream as Scrum |
